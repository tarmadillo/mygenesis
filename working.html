My name is Tony Armadillo. I am a freelance web designer born and raised in Corpus Christi. Building websites became a hobby for me back in 2008 while finishing my degree in Business Administration.

In 2010, after a short hiatus, I decided to go back to college, this time for electrical engineering. I graduated in 2014 with a Master of Science in Electrical Engineering from Texas A&M University Kingsville.

After graduation I was drawn back to web design this time with a renewed focus. I realized, while I greatly enjoy digital signal processing, VLSI and transistor design, I still love helping small and local businesses by improving their online presence. It’s likely because creating websites is a nice mix of business, programming and art.

As for hobbies, it’s difficult for me to define. I guess you could say my biggest hobby is reading or learning. I am currently teaching myself Ruby on Rails for building websites as a service. At the same time, I am also doing research on adaptive image compression to stay current on my electrical engineering.

I offer both theme customization and full-custom design services. Every site I build is easy to use and mobile responsive. Best of all, I provide basic training for free! Maintenance and Support

WordPress maintenance is essential to ensure your site runs properly, but it can be a chore for most people. Additionally, updating your site content can be time consuming if you are not accustomed to web development. I offer monthly maintenance and content update packages to save you the hassle.

Jekyll is GitHub's simple, blog-aware, static site generator. The best way to describe Jekyll without getting technical is that it takes a website template I create, combines it with the content you provide and "generates" static HTML files that can be run on just about any host provider.

The primary benefit is that there are no software or security updates for you to worry about. Therefore, monthly maintenance costs are practically non-existent. Since there is no database involved, static sites load far faster on average. The downside is that it will require a web developer such as myself to push any update to the website.
